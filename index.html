In order to prevent this epidemic issue it is important to understand who and why we are experiencing accelerating amounts of deaths. White Americans experience the highest overdose death rate of all reported races. This would suggest drug addiction is a primarily White issue, however, because of this sentiment, other ethnicities remain out of discussion regarding the treatment of opiate addiction, which may have led to an increase in overdose death rates among less represented communities in more recent years.

Men are about two times more likely to overdose from any drug. This is likely because men tend to take larger doses. In 2018 the per capita overdose death rate of all American men was 26.12 and 13.45 for women.

PREVENTION

CDC Link

This epidemic of drug overdoses is highly attributed to opioids, whether it be prescription opioids, or drugs such as synthetic opioids or heroin. The extreme dominance of drug overdoses being attributed to opiods may be due to the tripling of opiod drug prescriptions by U.S. pharmacies from 1991 to 2011. As well, drug overdoses have increased due to the splicing of drugs with fentanyl, and the major increase of the illegal drug trade's production flooding the United States market. This increase in opioid prescriptions and other illicit drugs in combination with the stigma surrounding those who do drugs as "bad people" makes it hard for someone who has developed an addiction to quit, as stigma affects one's self-esteem, the relationships between their friends and family, and prevents individuals suffering from addiction to acquire easy access to treatments. Raising awareness of how addictive and detrimental the drugs are to one's health is the key in slowing the spread of drug overdoses. If more individuals in the United States become conscientious of how serious this epidemic is, it could help slow the spread. The CDC recommends following these guidelines below:

1. Learn more about drug addiction to help people who are most at risk for drug use disorders and overdoses in your community.

2. Help those struggling with drug use disorders find the right care and treatment.

3. Increase awareness about drug abuse, addiction, and overdose by sharing Rx Awareness messages.
